? New to nuclear data? Quick definitions of half-life, decay modes, Bq and Q-values ▾

Half-life (T½)

The time it takes for half of a sample of a radioactive isotope to decay. After one more half-life, half of what remained decays too — and so on. Some isotopes have half-lives of microseconds; others, billions of years.

Decay modes

The way an unstable nucleus transforms to become more stable. α (alpha) emits a helium nucleus; β⁻ turns a neutron into a proton (emitting an electron); β⁺/EC turns a proton into a neutron; IT is a γ-ray "relaxation" from an excited isomer to the ground state.

Becquerel (Bq)

The SI unit of radioactivity: 1 Bq = one decay per second. "Specific activity" here is how many Bq are produced by one gram of the pure isotope — numbers range from a fraction of a Bq/g (near-stable) to over 1019 Bq/g (extremely short-lived).

Q-value (decay energy)

The total energy released in a single decay, in MeV (mega-electron-volts). It's shared between the emitted particle(s), the recoiling nucleus, and (for β decays) neutrinos.

Isomer (the "m" isotopes)

Some nuclides have a long-lived "excited" version with extra internal energy, written like 99mTc. It eventually drops to the normal ("ground state") nucleus, usually by emitting a γ-ray.

Scientific notation

Very large or small numbers are written as m × 10e — e.g. 1.95 × 1017 means 1.95 followed by 17 zeros.

Data Sources & Methodology

IAEA Live Chart of Nuclides
Ground-state half-lives, decay modes, branching ratios, atomic masses and Q-values for 3,383 nuclides (Z = 1–118), retrieved from the IAEA Nuclear Data Section API, which serves ENSDF/NUBASE-evaluated data.
www-nds.iaea.org/relnsd
Selected metastable isomers
Excitation energies, isomeric half-lives and branching (e.g. Tc-99m, Co-60m, Am-242m) from the IAEA nuclear level scheme database (ENSDF evaluations).
Cross-reference institutions
Underlying ENSDF evaluations are produced collaboratively by national labs and centres including NNDC (Brookhaven), IAEA-NDS, and the JRC-Geel (formerly JRC-ITU).
Specific activity is derived (not directly tabulated) using A = ln(2)·NA / (T½ · M), the standard relation used by NNDC/ORNL isotope reference sheets, where NA = 6.02214076×10²³ mol⁻¹. Decay energies are ground-state Q-values; for isomers, isomeric-transition energy equals the level excitation energy. Values shown in scientific notation. This tool is for educational reference — for regulatory or clinical use, consult primary IAEA/ENSDF evaluations directly.
